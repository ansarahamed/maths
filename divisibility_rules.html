📘 Divisibility Rules Notes

(Using correct mathematical terms for digit places)

🔹 1. Divisible by 2

Rule: A number is divisible by 2 if the digit at the units place is 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8.
Reason: These are even digits.

  • ✔️ Positive Example: 124 → units digit = 4 → ✅

  • ❌ Negative Example: 157 → units digit = 7 → ❌

🔹 2. Divisible by 5 and 10

✅ Divisible by 5

Rule: A number is divisible by 5 if the digit at the units place is 0 or 5.
Reason: All multiples of 5 end in 0 or 5.

  • ✔️ Example: 75 → units digit = 5 → ✅

  • ❌ Example: 77 → units digit = 7 → ❌

✅ Divisible by 10

Rule: A number is divisible by 10 if the digit at the units place is 0.
Reason: Every multiple of 10 ends in 0.

  • ✔️ Example: 130 → units digit = 0 → ✅

  • ❌ Example: 132 → units digit = 2 → ❌

🔹 3. Divisible by 3, 9, and 11

✅ Divisible by 3

Rule: If the sum of all digits is divisible by 3.
Reason: A pattern true for all multiples of 3.

  • ✔️ Example: 123 → 1+2+3 = 6 → ✅

  • ❌ Example: 124 → 1+2+4 = 7 → ❌

✅ Divisible by 9

Rule: If the sum of all digits is divisible by 9.
Reason: Similar to rule for 3, but stricter.

  • ✔️ Example: 153 → 1+5+3 = 9 → ✅

  • ❌ Example: 154 → 1+5+4 = 10 → ❌

✅ Divisible by 11

Rule: Find the difference between the sum of digits at odd positions and at even positions. If the result is 0 or divisible by 11, then the number is divisible by 11.
Reason: This pattern repeats for every 11th number.

  • ✔️ Example: 121 → (1 + 1) − 2 = 0 → ✅

  • ❌ Example: 123 → (1 + 3) − 2 = 2 → ❌

🔹 4. Divisible by 4 and 8

✅ Divisible by 4

Rule: A number is divisible by 4 if the number formed by the digits at the tens and units places is divisible by 4.
Reason: Any higher digits (like hundreds or thousands) are already multiples of 100, which is divisible by 4.

  • ✔️ Example: 316 → digits at tens and units = 1 and 6 → 16 ÷ 4 = 4 → ✅

  • ❌ Example: 318 → digits at tens and units = 1 and 8 → 18 ÷ 4 = 4.5 → ❌

✅ Divisible by 8

Rule: A number is divisible by 8 if the number formed by the digits at the hundreds, tens, and units places is divisible by 8.
Reason: All larger place values are already multiples of 1000 (which is divisible by 8).

  • ✔️ Example: 1000 → digits at hundreds, tens, units = 0, 0, 0 → 000 → ✅

  • ❌ Example: 125 → 125 ÷ 8 = 15.625 → ❌

🔹 5. Divisible by 6

Rule: A number is divisible by 6 if it is divisible by both 2 and 3.
Reason: 6 is the least common multiple (LCM) of 2 and 3.

  • ✔️ Example: 132 →

    • units digit = 2 → divisible by 2 ✅

    • sum = 1+3+2 = 6 → divisible by 3 ✅
      → ✅ Divisible by 6

  • ❌ Example: 134 → divisible by 2, but 1+3+4 = 8 → not divisible by 3 → ❌
